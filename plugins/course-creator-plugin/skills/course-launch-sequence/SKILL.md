# Course Launch Email Sequence

**Purpose:** Write a complete 10-email launch sequence for a course or program. The sequence builds anticipation, opens enrollment, handles objections, creates urgency, and closes with a final call.

## The 10-Email Structure

### Phase 1: Value & Warmup (Emails 1-3)

**Email 1: The Big Insight** (Day -3)
Give away a powerful, standalone insight related to the course topic. Pure value. No pitch. Build trust and demonstrate expertise.

**Email 2: The Problem** (Day -2)
Articulate the problem your course solves better than the reader can. Make them feel understood. Hint that you have a solution coming.

**Email 3: The Story** (Day -1)
Share your personal story — how you solved this problem for yourself. End with: "Tomorrow, I'm sharing something I've been working on..."

### Phase 2: Cart Open (Emails 4-5)

**Email 4: The Announcement** (Day 0 — Launch Day)
Course is open. Full details: what it is, what's included, the transformation, the price. Clear CTA to enroll. Excitement and clarity.

**Email 5: The Deep Dive** (Day 1)
Walk through the curriculum in detail. Module by module. Show them exactly what they'll learn and be able to do. Address "what's inside?"

### Phase 3: Social Proof & Objections (Emails 6-7)

**Email 6: Testimonials & Results** (Day 2)
Share student results, testimonials, or case studies. Let others do the selling. If launching for the first time, share your own results or beta feedback.

**Email 7: Objection Buster** (Day 3)
Address the top 3 objections directly:
- "Is this right for me?"
- "What if I don't have time?"
- "How is this different from X?"

### Phase 4: Urgency & Deadline (Emails 8-9)

**Email 8: The Deadline Reminder** (Day 5)
Enrollment is closing soon. Remind them what they'll miss. Recap the value. Last chance for bonus (if applicable).

**Email 9: Final Hours** (Day 6 — Last Day AM)
Doors close tonight. Emotional appeal — future pacing. "One year from now, you'll either have [result] or wish you started today."

### Phase 5: Final Call (Email 10)

**Email 10: Last Call** (Day 6 — Last Day PM, 2-4 hours before close)
Short. Direct. "This is it. Doors close at midnight. [Link]. I'd love to see you inside."

- [ ] Email 5 walks through curriculum
- [ ] Social proof is real or honestly stated as placeholder

- [ ] Urgency is genuine (real deadline, not fake scarcity)
- [ ] Email 10 is short and direct
- [ ] Voice matches context profiles throughout
