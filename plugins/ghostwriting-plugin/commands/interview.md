# Voice Capture Interview Generator

Generate a complete voice capture questionnaire that the user can send to their ghostwriting client. The questionnaire is designed to reveal the client's authentic voice, communication style, and content raw material.

## Instructions

1. Read context files (BUSINESS.md, ICP.md) if they exist for this client to customize the questionnaire
2. Generate the questionnaire below
3. Save it as a file the user can send to their client

## The Questionnaire

Generate a document with this structure:

```markdown
# Voice Capture Questionnaire

Hi [Client Name],

To write content that sounds authentically like you, I need to understand how you think, communicate, and see the world. This questionnaire will help me capture your voice so everything I write feels like it came from you.

**Instructions:**
- Answer in your natural voice — talk the way you actually talk
- Audio responses are even better than written ones (voice memos, Loom, etc.)
- Don't overthink it — first instinct answers are the most authentic
- Skip any question that doesn't apply, but try to answer most of them
- Estimated time: 30-45 minutes

---

## Section 1: How You Communicate

1. If you were explaining what you do to a stranger at a coffee shop, what would you say? (Keep it natural — not your LinkedIn headline)

2. When you're teaching someone something, do you tend to:
   a) Tell a story to illustrate the point
   b) Give them a step-by-step framework
   c) Share your opinion directly
   d) Show them data and evidence
   e) Use humor or provocation to make it memorable

3. Think of the last message or email you sent that you were really proud of. What made it good?

4. What words or phrases do you use ALL the time? (The ones your friends or colleagues would impersonate)

5. What words or phrases make you cringe? (Corporate jargon, buzzwords, anything that feels fake to you)

## Section 2: Your Opinions & Worldview

6. What's a belief you hold about your industry that most people disagree with?

7. What advice is commonly given in your space that you think is wrong or misleading?

8. What do you wish more people understood about [their field/topic]?

9. If you could change one thing about how your industry works, what would it be?

10. Complete this sentence: "The biggest mistake people make when it comes to [their topic] is ___"

## Section 3: Your Stories

11. What's your origin story? How did you get into what you do? (Not the polished version — the real one)

12. What's a failure or mistake that taught you something important? How do you usually tell that story?

13. What's a client win or result you're most proud of? Walk me through what happened.

14. What's a moment that changed how you think about your work?

15. Is there a story you find yourself telling over and over in conversations, presentations, or on calls?

## Section 4: Your Teaching Style

16. When you're explaining a complex concept, do you start with the big picture or the details?

17. Do you use analogies or metaphors? If so, what kinds? (Sports? Building? Cooking? Military?)

18. How do you feel about using data and statistics in your content? Love it, use it sometimes, or prefer to avoid it?

19. Do you prefer writing that's structured (headers, numbered lists, frameworks) or more conversational and flowing?

## Section 5: Your Content Preferences

20. Share 2-3 pieces of content from others that you wish you had written. What do you like about them?

21. Share 2-3 pieces of YOUR content that feel most "like you." What makes them work?

22. What kind of content do you never want to create? (Formats, tones, topics that are off-limits)

---

**Thank you!** Your answers will help me write content that sounds like it came straight from your brain. The more honest and natural your responses, the better the writing will be.

If you'd prefer to answer these over a call instead of writing, I'm happy to record and transcribe the conversation.
```

## Customization Rules

- Replace [Client Name] with actual name if known
- Replace [their field/topic] with specific references from BUSINESS.md
- If you know the client's industry, add 1-2 industry-specific questions
- If voice-capture will be done via call, add a note about scheduling
- Save as a clean document ready to share (email, Google Doc, Notion page)
