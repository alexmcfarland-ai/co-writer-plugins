# Content Pillars

**Purpose:** Define 3-5 strategic content pillars that organize all content production around the user's expertise, audience needs, and business goals. Pillars ensure every piece of content serves a strategic purpose.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- User wants to define or refine their content pillars
- User asks "what should I post about?" or "how do I organize my content?"
- User needs a strategic content framework
- User wants to stop creating content randomly

## What Makes a Good Pillar

A content pillar is NOT a topic — it's a strategic category. Each pillar should:
- **Map to expertise** — Something you can speak about with authority
- **Serve the ICP** — Addresses a real need your audience has
- **Support the business** — Leads toward your products/offers
- **Be inexhaustible** — Enough sub-topics for months of content
- **Be differentiated** — Your angle, not the generic industry take

## Process

### Step 1: Analyze the Intersection

**Identify pillars at the intersection of three circles:**
1. What the user is an expert in (from USER.md / BUSINESS.md)
2. What the ICP needs and cares about (from ICP.md)
3. What supports the user's business goals

### Step 2: Define 3-5 Pillars

For each pillar, provide:
- **Name** (short, memorable — 2-4 words)
- **Description** (one sentence — what this pillar covers)
- **Why it matters to the ICP** (connection to their pain/aspiration)
- **How it supports the business** (which product/offer it leads toward)
- **10 starter topics** (specific ideas within this pillar)
- **Best formats** (newsletter, thread, video, LinkedIn, etc.)
- **Recommended frequency** (how often to publish in this pillar)

### Step 3: Create the Pillar Map

Visual overview showing how pillars relate to each other and to the business.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] 3-5 pillars defined (not too few, not too many)
- [ ] Each pillar has a clear name and one-sentence description
- [ ] ICP connection is specific (not "they'll find it interesting")
- [ ] Business alignment is clear (each pillar leads somewhere)
- [ ] 10 specific topic ideas per pillar (not vague)
- [ ] Format recommendations match the pillar's strengths
- [ ] Frequency is realistic for the user's capacity
- [ ] Content mix recommendation included
- [ ] Pillar map shows relationships
- [ ] Anti-pillars defined (what NOT to post about)
