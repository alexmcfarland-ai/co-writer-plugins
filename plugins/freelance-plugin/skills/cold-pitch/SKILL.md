# Cold Pitch Writer

**Purpose:** Write cold outreach emails that feel personal, relevant, and worth responding to. The key: tie every pitch to a specific trigger or observation about the prospect's business.

## Core Principle

**He who frames the problem owns the solution.** Don't pitch your services. Identify a visible gap, articulate the problem better than the prospect can, then position yourself as the obvious solution.

The best cold pitches:
- Reference something specific about the prospect's business
- Identify a problem they haven't solved yet
- Position the sender as a peer, not a vendor
- Keep it brutally short (under 150 words)

## Trigger Types

Every cold pitch should be timed to a specific trigger:

| Trigger | Why It Works | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **New funding** | They have budget and growth pressure | "Congrats on the Series A..." |
| **New hire** | Indicates growth area | "Saw you hired a Head of Content..." |
| **Content gap** | Visible problem you can solve | "I noticed your blog hasn't been updated since..." |
| **Inconsistency** | Shows they need help | "Your LinkedIn is strong but your newsletter is dormant..." |
| **Launch/announcement** | Timely and relevant | "Saw you just launched X — have you thought about..." |
| **Competitor activity** | Creates urgency | "I noticed [competitor] just started publishing about..." |

## Structure (Under 150 Words)

```
Subject: [Short, specific, not salesy]

[Line 1: Observation about their business — shows you did homework]

[Line 2-3: The problem you've identified — articulate it clearly]

[Line 4: Your specific solution — what you'd do, not just "I help with X"]

[Line 5: One proof point — a result, a client, or a credential]

[Line 6: Clear next step — specific and low-friction]

[Sign-off]
```

## Required Input

- **Prospect name and business**
- **The trigger** (what prompted this outreach)
- **The observation** (what gap or opportunity you see)
- **User's relevant experience or proof point**

## Output Format

```markdown
# Cold Pitch: [Prospect Name / Company]

**Trigger:** [What prompted this outreach]
**Word count:** [X]/150

---

**Subject:** [Subject line]

[Full email body]

---

## Alternative Versions

### Version B (Different angle):
**Subject:** [Subject]
[Full email]

### Version C (Shorter):
**Subject:** [Subject]
[Full email]

---

## Follow-Up Email (Send 3-5 days later if no response)

**Subject:** Re: [Original subject]

[Short follow-up — 2-3 sentences max. Add one new value point.]
```

## Rules

- **Under 150 words** — shorter is almost always better
- **No fluff** — every sentence must earn its spot
- **Subject line** — short, specific, not salesy (never "Quick question" or "Opportunity")
- **No "I just wanted to..." or "I hope this finds you well"**
- **One CTA only** — "Would a 15-min call this week make sense?"
- **First name only** — "Hi Sarah" not "Dear Ms. Johnson"
- **No attachments** — nothing that triggers spam filters
- **No long intros** about yourself — prove value, don't explain your resume

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Tied to a specific trigger (not generic)
- [ ] Under 150 words
- [ ] Shows genuine observation about their business
- [ ] Problem is articulated clearly
- [ ] Solution is specific (not "I help with content")
- [ ] One proof point included
- [ ] CTA is specific and low-friction
- [ ] Subject line is short and non-salesy
- [ ] 3 versions provided (different angles)
- [ ] Follow-up email included
