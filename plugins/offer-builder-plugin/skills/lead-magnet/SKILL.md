# Lead Magnet Designer

**Purpose:** Design a complete lead magnet that solves one specific problem, recommends the best format, creates the content outline, and writes the landing page copy to convert visitors into subscribers.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- User wants to create a lead magnet or opt-in resource
- User mentions "freebie", "email list builder", "free resource"
- User wants to grow their email list
- User needs an entry point to their sales funnel

## Core Principle

**The best lead magnets solve one specific problem completely.** Not a sample. Not a teaser. A genuine solution to a real problem. When someone consumes your free resource and thinks "I can't believe this was free," they're primed to buy your paid offer.

**The hierarchy of perceived value:**
1. Something that costs you money to deliver (highest perceived value)
2. A tool, template, or resource they'll use repeatedly
3. A checklist or framework they can implement immediately
4. An educational guide or mini-course
5. A generic PDF (lowest perceived value)

## Process

### Step 1: Identify the Problem to Solve

From ICP.md and context, identify:
- The #1 most common problem the target audience faces
- A problem that naturally leads to the paid offer (strategic alignment)
- Something solvable in a single resource (not a full course worth of content)

### Step 2: Recommend Format

**Format options (ranked by effectiveness):**

| Format | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|--------|----------|------|------|
| **Template/Swipe File** | Creators, marketers, writers | Immediately usable, high perceived value | Requires real examples |
| **Checklist** | Process-oriented audiences | Fast to consume, actionable | Can feel thin if not detailed |
| **Mini-Course (5 emails)** | Complex topics | Builds relationship over time | Slower consumption |
| **Toolkit/Resource List** | Tool-heavy niches | High utility, bookmarkable | Can feel like a listicle |
| **Framework/Guide (PDF)** | Strategy-oriented audiences | Demonstrates expertise | Lower engagement if too long |
| **Calculator/Assessment** | Data-driven audiences | Interactive, personalized | Harder to create |

**Recommendation should be based on:**
- What the audience values most
- What demonstrates the creator's expertise
- What naturally leads to the paid offer

### Step 3: Create the Lead Magnet Outline

**Title:** Follow this formula — "[Specific Outcome] [Format]: [Qualifier]"
- Example: "The 5-Day Content System Blueprint: How to Create a Month of Content in One Sitting"
- Example: "The Freelancer Pricing Toolkit: Templates and Scripts for Charging What You're Worth"

**Content outline (5-7 sections):**
For each section:
- Section title
- What it covers (2-3 sentences)
- The key takeaway
- Any template, checklist, or tool included in that section

### Step 4: Write Landing Page Copy

**Landing page structure:**

1. **Headline** — States the specific outcome
2. **Subheadline** — Expands with "who it's for" or "how it works"
3. **3 Bullet Points** — What they'll get/learn/be able to do
4. **CTA** — "Get Free Access" / "Download Now" / "Send Me the [Resource]"
5. **Optional: Social proof** — "Join X,000+ people who've downloaded this"
