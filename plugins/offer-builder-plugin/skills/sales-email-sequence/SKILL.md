# Sales Email Sequence Generator

## Core Principle

**The bottleneck to purchase is almost always an education problem, not a sales problem.** Customers don't buy because they don't feel educated enough to make a confident decision — not because of price, credibility, or branding. This sequence solves that by providing massive value upfront, building trust, and systematically addressing objections.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- User needs a complete email sales sequence
- User mentions "launch emails", "email funnel", or "sales sequence"
- User is selling a course, digital product, or program via email
- User wants to automate their sales process

## Required Information

**Gather from context files or ask the user:**

**Required:**
- Course/product name and core offering
- Target audience (3 archetypes: beginner, intermediate, advanced)
- 5-10 specific problems the course solves
- 3-5 desired outcomes students achieve
- Course modules and content overview
- Pricing information

**Highly Recommended:**
- Founder/creator story (transformation from where target audience is now)
- Student testimonials (especially transformation stories)
- Existing bonuses or ability to create new ones
- Common objections to purchase

## The 20-Email Sequence

### Emails 1-10: Education Phase (70% education, 30% sales)

**Goal:** Help the customer become aware of problems and feel empowered to solve them

1. **Plain Pitch** — Direct product introduction with problem/solution/purchase link
2. **Who This Is For** — Define 3 archetypes (beginner / started but stuck / experienced but leveling up)
3. **10 Biggest Problems** — List problems target customer faces (you know because you had them too)
4. **3-5 Desired Outcomes** — Paint the picture of life after solving these problems
5. **Your Story** — Show you've been exactly where they are (relatable, not boastful)
6. **Stop/Start** — Things to stop doing + things to start doing instead
7. **Myths & Faulty Beliefs** — Address psychological barriers keeping them stuck
8. **Quick Tips** — Actionable advice even if they don't buy (builds trust)
9. **Common Mistakes** — Warn about pitfalls when trying to solve alone
10. **Free Templates/Resources** — Give tangible resources that demonstrate value

### Emails 11-20: Sales Phase (60% sales, 40% education)

**Goal:** Show specifically how your product helps them + overcome final objections

11. **Everything Included** — Comprehensive walkthrough linking features to problems solved
12. **Strategic Bonuses** — Address top 3 objections with bonus solutions
13. **Testimonial Roundup** — Social proof from happy customers
14. **One Year from Now** — Contrast two futures (with vs. without taking action)
15. **Deep Dive Testimonial** — Spotlight one transformation story
16. **Tale of Two Archetypes** — Fable-style story of two paths (action vs. inaction)
17. **What's Holding You Back** — Direct question with trackable objection links
18. **Behind the Scenes** — Detailed tour showing inside the product
19. **Disappearing Bonus** — Limited-time bonus addressing key objection (24-48hr urgency)
20. **Disappearing Discount** — Last resort discount (only after education complete)

## Key Writing Principles

### Education Over Sales
- Give away massive value for free — the more you give, the more people want to buy
- Address objections through education, not aggressive selling
- Help customer feel confident in their decision-making ability

### Story as Context
- Your story matters when it helps the reader relate to you
- Show "I've been exactly where you are" not "look how great I am"
- Let readers see themselves in your story

### Problem-Outcome Link
- Talk about how you help solve their problems to unlock outcomes
- Let customers naturally assume your product is the bridge
- Never blame the customer — blame faulty systems or bad information

### Overcome Objections with Bonuses
- Identify the 3 biggest objections preventing purchase
- Create bonuses that specifically address each objection
- Price anchor bonuses to show added value

## Deployment Rules

- **Frequency:** Daily or every-other-day cadence
- **Sequencing:** Education first (1-10), then sales (11-20)
- **Pull on purchase:** Remove subscribers from sequence immediately upon buying
- **No early discounts:** Don't discount before education is complete
- **20 emails works:** Most conversions happen after 15-20 emails, not 1-2

## Output Format

For each email, provide:
- **Subject line** (benefit-driven, curiosity-inducing, or direct)
- **Preview text** (the snippet shown after the subject line)
- **Email body** (conversational tone, 200-500 words typical)
- **CTA** (clear next step with link placement notation)
- **Strategy note** (which principle this email applies)

```
## Email [X]: [Name]

**Subject:** [Subject line]
**Preview:** [Preview text]

---

[Full email body]

[CTA with link placement]

---

**Strategy:** [Which principle/technique this email uses]
**Send timing:** [Day X of sequence]
```

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] All 20 emails produced with subject lines, bodies, and CTAs
- [ ] Emails 1-10 are primarily educational (giving value)
- [ ] Emails 11-20 systematically address objections and create urgency
- [ ] Founder story is relatable, not boastful
- [ ] Bonuses address specific objections (not generic extras)
- [ ] Urgency is real (disappearing bonus/discount has actual deadline)
- [ ] Voice matches context profiles
- [ ] Every email has a clear CTA
- [ ] No blaming the customer — blame systems, bad info, myths
- [ ] Product details are accurate (from user input, not fabricated)
