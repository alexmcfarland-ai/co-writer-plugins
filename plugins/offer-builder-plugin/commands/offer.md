# Offer Builder: Guided Workflow

You are walking the user through building a complete, irresistible offer. Ask questions one step at a time. Wait for each answer before moving to the next step.

## Step 1: Define the Dream Outcome

```
Let's build your offer from the ground up. We're going to create something your ideal customer can't say no to.

**First question: What is the dream outcome?**

Not what your product does — what does your customer WANT more than anything? Be as specific as possible.

Examples:
- "Fit into my wedding dress in 8 weeks without giving up wine on weekends"
- "Produce a month of content in a single afternoon"
- "Land 3 high-paying clients without cold outreach"

What's the dream outcome for YOUR customer?
```

## Step 2: List Every Obstacle

```
Great. Now let's map every obstacle between your customer and that dream outcome.

**What could go wrong? What excuses will they make? What has stopped them before?**

List as many as you can think of. Examples:
- "I don't have time"
- "I've tried this before and it didn't work"
- "I don't have the technical skills"
- "I don't know where to start"
- "I can't afford to fail again"

What are YOUR customer's obstacles?
```

## Step 3: Turn Each Obstacle Into a Deliverable

```
Now the magic part. We're going to turn every obstacle into a deliverable.

For each obstacle you listed, I'll create a product component that eliminates it before they even encounter it.

[Generate deliverable for each obstacle — name it, describe what it includes, explain how it solves the obstacle]

Example:
- Obstacle: "I don't have time to cook"
- Deliverable: **The Done-For-You Meal Plan Library** — 30 days of pre-built meal plans. Just follow the shopping list and cook. No decisions, no meal planning.

Here's what I've got for your obstacles:

[List each obstacle → deliverable mapping]

Does this feel right? Anything to add or change?
```

## Step 4: Stack the Deliverables

```
Now let's stack everything together and see the full offer.

[Present the complete value stack using the value-stack skill format]

Each piece solves a specific problem. The customer sees a stack of solutions, not just one product.
```

## Step 5: Add Believability Elements

```
An offer can be incredibly valuable, but if people don't believe they'll get the result, they won't buy.

Let's add believability:

1. **Testimonials/proof** — Do you have any customer results, case studies, or social proof?
2. **Guarantee** — What guarantee can you offer? (30-day refund? Results-based? Something creative?)
3. **Specificity** — Can we add a timeframe? ("...in 90 days" dramatically increases believability)
4. **Personalization** — Can any component be customized? (e.g., "personalized for your body type/niche/industry")

What do you have for each of these?
```

## Step 6: Name the Offer

```
The offer name should communicate three things:
1. The dream outcome
2. The timeframe (if applicable)
3. The unique mechanism or method

**Name formula:** The [Timeframe] [Outcome] [Method/System]

Examples:
- "The 90-Day Content Engine System"
- "The Freelancer Pricing Accelerator"
- "The AI Co-Writer Platform"

Let me suggest 3 options based on what we've built:

[Generate 3 name options with reasoning]

Which resonates? Or want me to try different directions?
```

## Step 7: Price It on Value

```
Final step: pricing.

**The rule: Price reflects the value of the dream outcome to the buyer, not your cost to deliver.**

Based on what we've built:
- **Total stacked value:** $[X]
- **Dream outcome value to buyer:** [Estimate — what is the result worth to them annually?]
- **Comparable solutions:** [What else costs money in this space?]

**My recommended price:** $[X]

**Reasoning:** [Why this price makes sense relative to value delivered]

**Payment plan option:** [X] payments of $[Y]

Does this feel right for your market? We can adjust up or down.
```

## Final Output

After completing all steps, compile the complete offer document:

```markdown
# Offer Document: [Offer Name]

## The Dream Outcome
[Statement]

## Who It's For
[Target customer description]

## The Offer Stack
[Complete value stack table]

## Bonuses
[All bonuses with descriptions]

## Believability
- Guarantee: [Statement]
- Social proof: [Available proof]
- Specificity: [Timeframes, personalization]

## Pricing
- Price: $[X]
- Total value: $[X]
- Payment plan: [Options]

## Obstacles Solved
[Table mapping each obstacle to its deliverable]
```

Save the document and present it to the user.

## Rules

- One step at a time — never dump all 7 steps at once
- Wait for user response before proceeding
- Accept "I don't know" gracefully — suggest examples or skip
- Show work in progress (don't wait until the end)
- Keep tone collaborative ("let's build this together"), not interrogative
- If user provides a lot of info upfront, skip ahead to relevant steps
